# The Mask Ceremony

Kenjiro stood at the edge of the courtyard, watching the dancers circle the fire. Each mask told a different story — the tiger spirit demanding justice, the crane offering mercy. He felt the old anger rising, the same fury his father carried from the detention camps. But there was something else too: a strange hope, fragile as paper, that the ritual could mend what history had broken.

The drum pattern shifted. The lead dancer — Tamia — turned toward him, her fox mask gleaming. She moved between two worlds, the world of the ancestors and the neon-lit streets of the present. Her dance was not nostalgia. It was a challenge.

"You cannot heal by forgetting," she whispered through the mask. "You heal by making the wound speak."
