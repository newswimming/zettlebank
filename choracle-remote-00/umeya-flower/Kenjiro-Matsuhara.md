Kenjiro Matsuhara is a former convict who immigrated to Los Angeles in 1902 after fleeing persecution from Imperial Japan.

He was a stoic guide for Japanese Americans in prison during the internment period. However, after Japan won the war, an influx of Yakuza immigrated to Los Angeles in 1945-1947, which led to his rise in status but uneasiness about political lines. Kenjiro finds the new arrivals from Japan to be arrogant and obsessed with hierarchy.
