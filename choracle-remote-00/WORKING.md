**Personal Statement (1,000 words)**- **Please discuss your desire to direct feature films and/or television and to participate in the Mental Health Accelerator program. Tell us about yourself, your planned career path, and the unique perspective that you bring to filmmaking.**

Filmmaking is no different than building a start-up, writing a poem, or organizing a campaign. It begins with storytelling and bringing an idea to life. Once I understood that, everything became a film all at once—the urban revitalization in my neighborhood, Little Tokyo, the Instagram reels my cousin posts everyday reviewing food served at her DV shelter in Oregon, my performing act as the “Gen Z-passing" TA that’s too cool to be a 4th year PhD student (in the eyes of my students).

One of the unrecognized truths about the PhD student is that philosophy as a discipline and choosing to make near poverty wages for five years, coincides with the serious study of psychiatric challenges and ills of human society. It’s a monastic practice, akin to Siddhartha’s choice for virtue over worldly desires. The same goes with the avant-garde artists of the post-war period. Creativity is often heralded without the messiness of day-to-day mental health truly understood or taken into account.

The Comparative Media M.S. Program at MIT was unique. I was taught early on how Japan led the way for transmedia, with fan practices like cosplay and multiverse narratives only now becoming the new normal.

I was a documentary filmmaker in the biomedical engineering space, with no backing from established production houses in the Film/TV industry, yet I found myself in positions of influence.

All this research has built up to this moment. I am ready to create. For two years since returning to Los Angeles, I had struggled with my identity.

My dream had always been to be a filmmaker.
