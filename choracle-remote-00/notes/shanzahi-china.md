### **1. Shenzhen** – _The Heart of Shanzhai & Hardware Innovation_

- **Huaqiangbei Electronics Market (华强北)**: The world’s largest electronics market, where shanzhai smartphones, components, and DIY electronics are traded.
- **Seeed Studio & Chaihuo Maker Space (柴火创客空间)**: One of China’s first makerspaces, supporting open-source hardware innovation.
- **HAX Accelerator**: A hardware startup incubator helping global makers turn prototypes into products.
- **Huaqiangbei Factories**: Many small-scale factories specialize in rapid prototyping and knockoff or rebranded consumer tech.

### **2. Dongguan** – _Manufacturing & Hardware Experimentation_

- A hub for rapid prototyping and low-cost manufacturing, supporting the shanzhai ecosystem.
- Home to large-scale contract manufacturers that produce knockoffs and clones of mainstream products.
- Many migrant worker-led grassroots innovations emerge from factory floor knowledge.

### **3. Guangzhou** – _Consumer Markets & Shanzhai Distribution_

- **Baima Clothing Market (白马服装市场)**: A hub for counterfeit and imitation fashion brands.
- **Zhanxi Road & Tianhe Markets**: Areas known for fake luxury goods and knockoff electronics.

### **4. Yiwu** – _Small Commodities & Copycat Goods_

- **Yiwu International Trade City**: The world’s largest wholesale market for small goods, where shanzhai products ranging from toys to electronics are mass-distributed.

### **5. Chengdu & Chongqing** – _Emerging Maker & Hacker Communities_

- Cities with strong gaming, software, and hardware hacking cultures.
- **Chengdu Makerspace**: A growing community of DIY hardware and software innovators.

### **6. Beijing & Shanghai** – _Tech & DIY Culture_

- **Beijing’s Zhongguancun (中关村)**: Known as "China’s Silicon Valley," it has a strong startup scene with hacker culture influence.
- **XinCheJian (新车间) in Shanghai**: One of the first makerspaces in China, focusing on open-source hardware.

### Water

The opening episode in the novel is the release of the [108 Spirits](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/108_Stars_of_Destiny "108 Stars of Destiny"), imprisoned under an ancient [stele-bearing tortoise](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bixi_\(tortoise\) "Bixi (tortoise)").[[9]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Water_Margin#cite_note-10)

The 108 Heroes (一百单八将) are at the core of the plot of Water Margin. Based on the Taoist concept that each person's destiny is tied to a "Star of Destiny" (宿星), the 108 Stars of Destiny are stars representing 108 demonic overlords who were banished by the deity Shangdi. Having repented since their expulsion, the 108 Stars are accidentally released from their place of confinement, and are reborn in the world as 108 heroes who band together for the cause of justice. They are divided into the 36 Heavenly Spirits and 72 Earthly Fiends.
