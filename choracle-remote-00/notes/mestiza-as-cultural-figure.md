

The mestiza herself is a product of cultural memory; her body too is mapped by racialized and gendered practices of individual and collective identity

European and indigenous, betwixt and between, central to Mexico's national identity yet politically marginalized. What does mestiza mean, though, in relation to cultural memory? 
- not blood, but performance: manner, dress, style, language, religion, and setting
- codified system of identification

the Intermediary refers to her heart as a cautious lover knocking to come in, or a chick pecking at the walls of its egg, as a sea anemmone and an ever-powerful flower

The Intermediary, then, exists in a constant state of transformation. She metamorphises from the pueblo woman in dark, rustic clothing to a miraculous apparition, one more in the long line of goddess/virgin figures of mixed indigenous and European origins
