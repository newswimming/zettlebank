Chris Milk  - "Empathy Machine"
- VR is an experiential medium
- Media's power to create visceral reactions and effect change 
- MIlk's rhetoric is not unique to virtual reality, but the "empathy machine" rhetoric is a unique conflation if technological determinism with white guilt; the machine makes us more compassioante, empathetic, and connected
- "human to human connection is deeper in VR" is false; VR is machine that makes us more human

As Saidiya Hartman, a scholar of African American literature and history, writes of documentation around the Transatlantic slave trade, “it becomes clear that empathy is double-edged, for in making the other's suffering one's own, this suffering is occluded by the other's obliteration” [[39](https://dl.acm.org/doi/fullHtml/10.1145/3290605.3300528#bib39), p. 20].
