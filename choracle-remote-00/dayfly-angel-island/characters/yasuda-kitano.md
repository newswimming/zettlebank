
Yasuda Kitano first emigrated to California in 1904, when he was 25-years-old. He worked at the train stations and eventually found a position as a janitor at the Christian mission schools. 

Yasuda fell into a gambling addiction and lost most of his earnings. Though he was of an early generation of immigrants, he was unable to significantly improve his career outcomes over the decade he was residing in Los Angeles. He eventually found residence at a boarding house in Weller Street near the rice cake factory where he worker. Yasuda was well known by the shopkeepers and worker men because he often loitered in his free time.

Through a favor with one of his mates, Yasuda was able to join a registry that facilitates "picture brides" under the auspice of being a business owner. 

Yasuda was 38-years-old when he married 20-year-old Motome Kimura.
