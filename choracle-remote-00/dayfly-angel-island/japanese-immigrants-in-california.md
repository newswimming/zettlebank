
**California:** The first recorded settlement of Japanese immigrants in California was the Wakamatsu Tea and Silk Farm Colony, established near Sacramento in **1869**, shortly after the Meiji Restoration.

The first _major wave_ of Japanese immigration to the West Coast began in the **1880s and 1890s**. This influx was largely driven by the Chinese Exclusion Act of 1882, which created a massive labor shortage in agriculture, domestic service, and railroad construction that Japanese immigrants stepped in to fill.

**Los Angeles:** While Japanese immigrants began settling in Los Angeles in the late 19th century, the city saw a massive, defining surge in **1906**. Following the devastating San Francisco earthquake and the ensuing spike in anti-Asian hostility in Northern California, roughly 2,000 to 3,000 Japanese immigrants migrated south to Los Angeles. This migration solidified the foundations of communities like Little Tokyo on East Alameda and the vibrant fishing village on Terminal Island.
